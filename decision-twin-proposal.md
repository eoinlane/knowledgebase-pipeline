# Dublin Citiverse: A Reference City for the ITU Global Initiative

## Executive Summary

The International Telecommunication Union (ITU), in partnership with UNICC and Digital Dubai, has launched the **Global Initiative on Virtual Worlds and AI — Discovering the Citiverse**: a UN-backed framework to build the digital public infrastructure of future cities. The first UN Citiverse Challenge is open now.

This proposal positions **Dublin as a reference implementation city** for the Citiverse — contributing a working model of AI-powered city infrastructure built on open, interoperable standards. Rather than another pilot, Dublin would deliver a live Digital Decision Twin with real use cases across transport, planning, and public safety, in partnership with leading European smart cities and anchored to an internationally recognised framework.

---

## The Problem We Are Solving

88% of cities are running AI pilots. Only 7% reach enterprise deployment.

The gap is not technology — it is infrastructure. Cities lack a shared substrate: a trusted, interoperable layer that connects data across departments and lets AI insight flow into operational action. Every department builds in isolation. Every pilot stays a pilot.

The ITU Citiverse initiative exists to solve this at a global standards level. Dublin has the institutional partners, the live use cases, and the connections to European peer cities to make it real.

---

## The Concept: City Verse as Substrate

The **City Verse** is Dublin's implementation of the Citiverse substrate — an open digital infrastructure layer connecting city data, services, and AI applications across DCC, NTA, DFB, and other city actors. It is not a product. It is the foundation on which city-scale AI becomes possible.

Built on ITU interoperability standards, the City Verse enables:

- Any department to publish and consume data through a common layer
- AI applications to act on live city data, not curated pilot datasets
- Decisions to flow from insight to action without manual handoffs
- Independent applications (transport, planning, emergency response) to share context

Rotterdam built their version of this over 10 years. They launched in October 2024. Dublin can move faster by building on what they learned.

## Dublin Use Cases

Three live use cases, each a deployable application sitting on top of the City Verse substrate. Each one addresses the 7% problem: a clear path from POC to enterprise deployment.

---

### Use Case 1 — NTA: Intelligent Transport Operations

The NTA already has four AI POCs in flight: PQ automation, ad-hoc bus licensing, taxi compliance case management, and contract variations. These are strong individual pilots. The City Verse substrate transforms them from isolated tools into a connected transport intelligence layer.

**What it looks like on the substrate:**

- Real-time GTFS data, contract variation records, and licensing data published to the City Verse layer
- AI agents (already prototyped) query live city data rather than static exports
- Decisions — route approvals, variation sign-offs, PQ responses — flow from AI insight directly to NTA operational systems
- The transport layer becomes available to other city actors: DFB routing, DCC planning, event management

**Why the Citiverse framing matters for NTA:** The four POCs are currently presented as separate business cases going through individual governance gates. Framing them as the transport layer of a UN-aligned city infrastructure initiative elevates the programme — from "AI tools" to "national digital infrastructure" — and opens funding routes beyond the current budget envelope.

---

### Use Case 2 — DCC: Building Control & BIM

The Building Control and BIM work represents Dublin's most mature digital planning data. A BIM layer on the City Verse substrate connects planning applications, inspection records, and engineering models into a live decision environment.

**What it looks like on the substrate:**

- BIM models and planning data published as city substrate assets
- Inspectors access live, connected records rather than siloed document stores
- Bentley's digital twin infrastructure provides the 3D/spatial layer
- Google's geospatial platform provides the city-wide spatial context

---

### Use Case 3 — DFB: Emergency Response Intelligence

Rob Howell's operational data is one of the most compelling smart city use cases available — and one of the least explored in Dublin. Emergency response is time-critical, data-dependent, and currently operating largely on institutional knowledge and legacy dispatch systems.

**What it looks like on the substrate:**

- DFB operational data connected to real-time city layers (traffic, events, building data from BIM, water/utility infrastructure)
- AI-driven resourcing recommendations informed by live city context
- Incident routing informed by BIM data (building access, hazardous materials, occupancy)
- Clear, measurable public safety outcome

## Programme Structure

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Q2 2026)

- Register Dublin with the ITU Citiverse Assembly as a reference city
- Establish bilateral relationships with Roland van der Heijden, Max van Meerten, and Teppo Rantanen
- Workshop with DCC and NTA to confirm use case priority and substrate architecture
- Engage Bentley and Google on technical design
- **Output:** City Verse architecture document + partner MOU

### Phase 2 — European Citiverse Hackathon (Q3 2026)

- Cross-city event bringing Dublin, Rotterdam, The Hague, and Tampere together
- Each city team builds on a shared substrate instance
- Bentley and Google as technical sponsors; Jacobs as engineering advisory
- Cities Today Institute publishes the outputs internationally
- ITU Citiverse Assembly recognition for participating cities
- **Output:** Working prototype across all three use cases + published case study

### Phase 3 — Reference Implementation (Q4 2026–2027)

- Full deployment of transport layer (NTA) as the anchor use case
- BIM/Building Control layer as second deployment
- DFB response intelligence as third
- Dublin formally submitted as an ITU Citiverse reference city
- Noval Consultancy as programme delivery lead

## Why Dublin, Why Now

- Roland van der Heijden's open platform is new and Rotterdam is actively seeking partner cities — Dublin can co-shape the model rather than inherit it
- NTA's four POCs are at exactly the point where they either progress to infrastructure or stall as pilots — the Citiverse framing provides the escalation path
- The ITU Citiverse Challenge is open now — early movers gain disproportionate visibility and shape the standards
- DCC's BIM work, DFB's operational data, and NTA's transport POCs together represent a more complete and compelling city dataset than most participating cities can offer
